Stansted Airport Drop-Off Charges and Rules Updated for 2025

Plan your drop-off at Stansted Airport with the latest fees, free options, and essential tips to save time and money.

Why Stansted Airport Charges a Drop-Off Fee

London Stansted Airport is one of the UK’s busiest hubs, serving millions of passengers each year. Whether you’re dropping off friends, family, or clients, understanding the airport’s current drop-off charges and rules can save time, money, and frustration. Stansted introduced its Express Set Down fee to keep traffic moving and fund site improvements. The charge discourages vehicles from lingering at the terminal, reducing congestion and emissions. By keeping the flow efficient, passengers benefit from quicker access to departures, while drivers avoid gridlock.

Express Set Down Zone – Closest to the Terminal

The Express Set Down zone is the most convenient place to drop passengers directly outside the terminal.

  • Price 2025: £7 for up to 15 minutes
  • Stay Beyond 15 Minutes: £25 flat charge
  • Return Within 30 Minutes: £20 re-entry fee

Drivers enter via the main terminal road, follow clear signs to “Express Set Down,” and pay at the exit barrier with card or contactless. There is no cash option. Tips: Aim to unload swiftly—have bags ready and say quick goodbyes. Avoid idling or waiting for passengers to exit security.

Free Drop-Off Option – Mid-Stay Car Park

If you prefer to avoid fees, the Mid-Stay Car Park is the best alternative. It offers 60 minutes of free parking, with a shuttle bus running every 10 minutes to the terminal (journey time around 7 minutes). After the first hour, normal mid-stay parking rates apply. Ideal for families or those assisting passengers with heavy luggage. Tip: Plan an extra 15–20 minutes to accommodate shuttle timing.

Short-Stay Car Parks for Longer Goodbyes

For those wanting to accompany passengers inside or wait for a confirmed flight, the Short-Stay Car Parks (Orange, Blue, Green zones) offer the convenience of being within walking distance of the terminal. Charges start around £7–£10 for 30 minutes (rates vary by zone). Perfect for meeting arriving travellers or if you need more time than Express Set Down allows.

Disabled Access & Blue Badge Holders

Stansted provides designated disabled bays within the Express Set Down area and Short-Stay Car Parks. Blue Badge holders still pay the standard tariff but can use wider bays and benefit from easier terminal access. If you need assistance, you can pre-book support through the airport’s special assistance service.

Key Rules and Restrictions

  • No waiting or idling: You must actively unload and leave.
  • Camera enforcement: Automatic number plate recognition (ANPR) tracks time precisely.
  • No walking from Mid-Stay: Passengers must use the shuttle service for safety.
  • No cash payment: Card or contactless only at exit barriers.

Planning Tips to Save Time and Money

  • Pre-plan drop-off: Know whether you’ll use Express, Mid-Stay, or Short-Stay before you arrive.
  • Check traffic: The M11 and A120 can be busy during peak hours; allow extra time.
  • Use ride-sharing apps or licensed minicabs: Pre-booked taxis can reduce the need for multiple family cars.

Comparing Stansted to Other London Airports

Stansted’s £7 fee is similar to Gatwick’s and slightly less than Heathrow’s terminal drop-off charge. All London airports now use drop-off fees to manage congestion, so the concept is standard across the region.

FAQs

Q1: Can I drop off passengers for free at Stansted?
Yes, the Mid-Stay Car Park allows up to 60 minutes free, with a fast shuttle to the terminal.

Q2: What happens if I exceed 15 minutes in Express Set Down?
You’ll be charged a flat £25 at the exit barrier.

Q3: Is there a penalty for returning quickly?
Re-entering within 30 minutes incurs an additional £20 fee.

Q4: Are motorcycles charged?
Yes, motorcycles pay the same tariff as cars.

Q5: Can I pay with cash?
No, only card or contactless payments are accepted.

Key Takeaways

For fastest access, use the Express Set Down zone and keep your stop under 15 minutes for a £7 charge. For a free option, head to the Mid-Stay Car Park and factor in the shuttle time. For longer stays, choose a Short-Stay Car Park. By planning ahead, you’ll avoid unnecessary fees and make the trip stress-free.
